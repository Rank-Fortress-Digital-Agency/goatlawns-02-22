How to Keep Your Lawn Looking Great All Summer


The weather is heating up, and that means it’s time to start thinking about how to keep your lawn looking great all summer long! Follow these tips and you’ll be able to enjoy a lush, green lawn all season long.

First, make sure you are watering your lawn correctly. Most lawns need about an inch of water per week, but you’ll need to adjust your watering depending on the type of grass you have and the weather conditions. You can use a rain gauge or soil moisture meter to help you determine how much water your lawn needs.

Second, fertilize your lawn regularly. A good fertilizer will help your lawn stay healthy and green all summer long. Be sure to follow the instructions on the package, and never apply fertilizer to a dry lawn.

Third, mow your lawn regularly. Mowing helps keep your grass at the correct height, which in turn will help it grow thicker and healthier. Be sure to leave at least two inches of grass when you mow so that the roots can grow deep into the soil where they’ll get more nutrients.

If your lawn has been dormant during the winter, you’ll need to do a little bit of work to get it ready for spring. The first step is to remove any dead grass or leaves. You can do this either by hand or with a rake. Once the dead grass is gone, you can begin to prepare your lawn for the growing season.

You’ll want to make sure that there is no standing water on the ground, and you should remove any rocks or other debris that could cause damage to your lawn equipment. If you have grass seed in your yard, it’s time to plant new seeds and start watering them. You don’t need to do anything else at this point; the new grass will start to grow on its own.

What is fertilization and why is it important for plants and gardens?


In order to have a healthy, thriving garden, it’s important to provide your plants with the right nutrients. Fertilization is one way to do this – by providing your plants with a balanced diet of nitrogen, phosphorus and potassium (NPK), you help them grow big and strong.

There are a few different ways to fertilize your plants. One is to use organic fertilizers, which are made from natural ingredients like compost or manure. Another is to use inorganic fertilizers, which are made from synthetic ingredients. Inorganic fertilizers are often faster-acting than organic ones, but they can also be more harmful to the environment.

No matter which type of fertilizer you choose, it’s important to follow instructions carefully and apply them at the right time of year (and not too often!). When you fertilize your plants, be sure to do it near their roots so that they can absorb nutrients directly from the soil. You don’t want any fertilizer on top or else it might burn out before reaching down where it needs to go!
